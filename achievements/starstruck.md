# ⭐ Starstruck

> Have a repository receive a high number of stars.

**Difficulty:** 🔴 Hard  
**Tiers:** 4 (Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum)  
**Time to earn:** Depends on your project's popularity

---

## 🏆 Tiers

| Tier | Stars on one repo |
|------|------------------|
| 🥉 Bronze (x2) | 16 |
| 🥈 Silver (x3) | 128 |
| 🥇 Gold (x4) | 512 |
| 💎 Platinum (x5) | 4096 |

---

## 📋 How to Earn It

1. Build something **useful or interesting**
2. Make the repo **public**
3. Write a great **README.md** with clear description and instructions
4. **Share it** with the world!

---

## 💡 Ideas for Starrable Projects

- A useful **Python script or tool**
- A **resource list** (like this repo!)
- A **tutorial or guide** on a popular topic
- A **fun project** people would want to try

---

## 📢 Where to Share Your Repo

| Platform | How |
|----------|-----|
| Reddit | Post in r/Python, r/programming, r/github |
| Twitter/X | Tweet with relevant hashtags |
| LinkedIn | Share as a post |
| Dev.to | Write an article about your project |
| Hacker News | Submit to Show HN |

---

## ✅ Tips

- A **great README** is the #1 factor — add screenshots, badges, and clear instructions
- Add relevant **topics/tags** to your repo so people can discover it
- Solve a **real problem** people have
- Engage with people who open issues or PRs

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

- Making the repo **private** — must be public
- Poor README with no explanation of what the project does
- Not sharing it anywhere — people can't star what they can't find!
